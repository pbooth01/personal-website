Texty-Jukebox

Allow your night to be powered by the people who matter most.

Text Me

Here's the scene: it’s Friday night, you’ve got friends over, and everyone wants to listen to some great music. The problem? Everyone wants to jam to something different, and you’re not thrilled to sit by your laptop all night. Enter, the Txty-Jukebox.

Txty-Jukebox generates you a unique number your guests text song requests into. As TxtyJukebox receives song requests, it searches YouTube for music videos and then places the videos into your event’s queue. If you hook up TxtyJukebox to a TV you’ll be able to jam to videos on a big screen with big room sound all night long.

Simplicity is Key

1.

You create an event and we give you a phone number.

2.

People text in song requests to the number.

3.

Requests automagically queue up YouTube videos.

Sometimes it feels like you need to download an app to do anything.

The driving force behind this application was ACCESSIBILITY. Everywhere you turn it seems like you are being pestered to download somebody's app, no matter how small the service.

I wanted to build something that could be used by anyone seamlessly. Your cell phone is a ubiquitous part of your life so what’s simpler than texting in your next song request? Since texting is second nature, it was the logical choice for an easy and accessible medium to build the application around.

The Nitty Gritty

The webapp itself is a standard Symfony2 app along with the usual suspects – Bootstrap, Underscore, and a sprinkling of jQuery. It uses Twillio’s REST API to handle SMS along with a “webhook” from Twillio to the webapp to recieve messages. In addition, it leverages the YouTube API to search and load videos that are then loaded into an iframe.
