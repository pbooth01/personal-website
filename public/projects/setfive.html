Setfive Summer

My time at Setfive.

Life is about more than just C++

For the last year, I've been lucky enough to be a part of the engineering team at Setfive Consulting.

I started in the summer of 2015, and was lucky enough to be able to stay on while I finished out my computer science degree at Tufts.

I learned a ton during that time and made the transition from a C / C++ developer to a web engineer, one comfortable working with any part of the stack. In addition to improving my software development skills I was able to gain an in depth understanding of web technologies, and how these technologies fit together to build large scale applications.

What I worked on.

Since Setfive is a technology consulting firm they build out technological solutions for a multitude clients. I was given the chance to work on a large number of projects ranging all over the board.

I started my time at Setfive working underneath one of the senior level engineers, writing integration tests, and moved on to building out technology for clients.

Here is some of the stuff I worked on.

Pspeak.com

My first client project was Pspeak.com, an online public speaking training platform. I worked on the frontend of the site and was also tasked with updating backend code to handle pricing and making some improvements to their system that handled cycling coupon codes for users.

In building the site I had the opportunity to work alongside both designers and engineers. It was an incredibly rewarding experince that taught me a lot about working in a team setting, and effectivley communicating with all members of a cross functional team.

It's very easy to forget that not everyone is an engineer and knows the differnence between DOM manipulation using JQuery and a CSS transition. When working on a team being able to ask the right questions and help someone understand the technical solutions that you are implementing to bring their ideas to frution is just as, if not more important, than writing the code itself.

CVET

After working on Pspeak I was tasked with building out a brochure site for one of our clients CVET, a software company that acts a s a platform for quantifying the goods and services being offered by businesses. The site was in need of some serious structure, and needed to be built in a way that would allow for additional content to be added painlessly in the future.

In order to give the site some structure I decided to switch it over to the Sillex web framework. I chose Sillex becuse it was a PHP based micro-frame work made up of Symfony components so any one of the other engineers in the office could easily make upates if need be. I decided that becuse the site was only static content, and didn't require and ORM or any way to connect to a database that the full blown Symfony2 framework would require too much overhead.

In addition to the structural changes I added some updates to make the site more mobile responsive.
