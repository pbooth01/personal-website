Chowsr.com

Because making those late night food descions is always the hardest.

As a college student late nights were part of my everyday life. Whether it was cramming for a test or killing time on Reddit, my friends and I often found ourselves up late into the night and in need of food to keep us going.

By my sophomore year I realized how much indecision went into food decisions, so I got 4 of my engineering friends together to build Chowsr.com.

The goal of the site was very simple: Give people and their friends a way to not just know what food was available around them, but to actually make a decision without wasting hours deliberating.

Features

The site offered users interesting ways to select from the food around them:

1) ChowBrow, Netflix style browsing of local food.

2) Swipester, a Tinder inspired suggestion interface.

3) Wheel of Food, a wheel full of the highest rated food around you.

4) Food Bracket, a March Madness inspired selection. It pits restaurants against one another to leave you with a winner.
